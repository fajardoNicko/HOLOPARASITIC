# Parasite-Profile Validation — Reference List

References that back the biological **input assumptions** of the multi-parasite
comparison (`run_parasites.py`, ANALYSIS.md §3.7): each parasite's **attachment
site** (stem / root / systemic) and **trophic mode** (holoparasite vs hemiparasite).
The study focuses on **holoparasites** spanning the three attachment geometries;
the two hemiparasites are a comparison only. All DOIs below were verified against
publisher / PubMed / journal pages during a web search (none invented). **OA** =
open access.

> Scope note: these validate the *inputs* (where each parasite attaches and how
> dependent it is), **not** the model's p_c numbers — no empirical "vascular
> percolation threshold" exists for any parasite. This is the same framework-first
> stance used throughout `VALIDATION.md`.

---

## A. The classification framework (root/stem × holo/hemi)

These justify the two-axis scheme the whole comparison is built on.

1. **Těšitel, J. (2016).** Functional biology of parasitic plants: a review.
   *Plant Ecology and Evolution*, 149(1), 5–20. DOI: **10.5091/plecevo.2016.1097**.
   **OA (CC BY).** → *Strongest single source for the attachment × trophic-mode
   classification and the holo-vs-hemi resource-flow contrast.*
2. **Twyford, A. D. (2018).** Parasitic plants. *Current Biology*, 28(16),
   R857–R859. DOI: **10.1016/j.cub.2018.06.030**. (Primer; freely accessible.)
   → Concise; explicitly names *Cuscuta*, *Orobanche*, *Striga*, mistletoe in the
   hemi/holo and stem/root scheme.
3. **Nickrent, D. L. (2020).** Parasitic angiosperms: how often and how many?
   *Taxon*, 69(1), 5–27. DOI: **10.1002/tax.12195**. (Paywalled; author copy on
   nickrentlab.siu.edu.) → Definitive census (12 origins, ~4,750 species); places
   all four lineages in the classification.
4. **Westwood, J. H., Yoder, J. I., Timko, M. P., & dePamphilis, C. W. (2010).**
   The evolution of parasitism in plants. *Trends in Plant Science*, 15(4),
   227–235. DOI: **10.1016/j.tplants.2010.01.004**. (Paywalled; author PDF at UC
   Davis.) → Facultative→obligate dependence continuum; haustorial transfer.
   *(Note: cite as Westwood/Yoder/Timko/dePamphilis — NOT Musselman.)*
5. **Yoshida, S., Cui, S., Ichihashi, Y., & Shirasu, K. (2016).** The haustorium,
   a specialized invasive organ in parasitic plants. *Annual Review of Plant
   Biology*, 67, 643–667. DOI: **10.1146/annurev-arplant-043015-111702**.
   (Paywalled.) → The haustorium "penetrates into the host **stem or root** and
   connects to its vasculature" — backs the attachment-site axis + xylem bridge.

## C. *Orobanche* / *Phelipanche* (broomrape) — root holoparasite (xylem + phloem)

7. **Shilo, T., Rubin, B., Plakhine, D., Gal, S., Amir, R., Hacham, Y., Wolf, S.,
   & Eizenberg, H. (2017).** Secondary effects of glyphosate action in
   *Phelipanche aegyptiaca*: inhibition of solute transport from the host plant to
   the parasite. *Frontiers in Plant Science*, 8, 255.
   DOI: **10.3389/fpls.2017.00255**. **OA.** → *Strongest single source* — states
   obligate **root** parasitism, **holo**parasitism (entire dependence on host for
   water, carbohydrates, nutrients), and **xylem + phloem** haustorial connection.
8. **Joel, D. M., Gressel, J., & Musselman, L. J. (Eds.) (2013).** *Parasitic
   Orobanchaceae: Parasitic Mechanisms and Control Strategies.* Springer.
   DOI (book): **10.1007/978-3-642-38146-1**. (Paywalled.) → Standard monograph;
   cite a specific chapter (e.g., Joel, "The Haustorium and the Life Cycles of
   Parasitic Orobanchaceae") for haustorium structure / xylem–phloem bridging.

## D. *Striga* (witchweed) — root hemiparasite (photosynthetic; xylem)

9. **Spallek, T., Mutuku, M., & Shirasu, K. (2013).** The genus *Striga*: a witch
   profile. *Molecular Plant Pathology*, 14(9), 861–869.
   DOI: **10.1111/mpp.12058**. **OA (PMC6638688).** → *Strongest single source* —
   verbatim: obligate **root** hemiparasite; photosynthetic but **host-dependent**
   (negative carbon gain); **xylem-to-xylem** connection (no phloem connection);
   impact figures (~50 M ha affected; >100 M people; ~US$1 B/yr; 20–80% losses).
10. **Runo, S., & Kuria, E. K. (2018).** Habits of a highly successful cereal
    killer, *Striga*. *PLoS Pathogens*, 14(1), e1006731.
    DOI: **10.1371/journal.ppat.1006731**. **OA (CC BY).** → Root hemiparasite of
    cereals; sub-Saharan impact.
11. **Scholes, J. D., & Press, M. C. (2008).** *Striga* infestation of cereal
    crops — an unsolved problem in resource limited agriculture. *Current Opinion
    in Plant Biology*, 11(2), 180–186. DOI: **10.1016/j.pbi.2008.02.004**.
    (Paywalled.) → Authoritative review context.
12. **Parker, C. (2009).** Observations on the current status of *Orobanche* and
    *Striga* problems worldwide. *Pest Management Science*, 65(5), 453–459.
    DOI: **10.1002/ps.1713**. (Paywalled.) → Worldwide impact of *both* root
    parasites (use for scale; notes reliable figures are scarce).

## E. Mistletoe — aerial stem hemiparasite (xylem; water sink)

13. **Glatzel, G., & Geils, B. W. (2009).** Mistletoe ecophysiology:
    host–parasite interactions. *Botany*, 87(1), 10–15. DOI: **10.1139/B08-096**.
    (Free PDF via USDA Forest Service Treesearch #34006.) → Aerial hemiparasite;
    taps host **xylem**; high transpiration / more-negative water potential —
    backs the "moderate water sink, stem attachment" profile.

---

## F. *Pilostyles* / endophytic holoparasites — systemic (diffuse interface)

14. **Nickrent, D. L. (2020).** *see A3* — the census places the endophytic
    holoparasite families (Apodanthaceae, incl. *Pilostyles*; Rafflesiaceae) among
    parasitic angiosperms; these live almost entirely *inside* the host as
    endophytic strands, emerging only to flower → backs a **systemic/distributed**
    (not backbone-localised) vascular interface.
15. **Těšitel, J. (2016).** *see A1* — functional-biology basis for endophytic
    holoparasitism (total host dependence; diffuse internal host contact).

> Honest scope: *Pilostyles* is included to exercise the **systemic/distributed**
> attachment geometry (modelled as "random" tapping), **not** as a crop pest.
> Quantified vascular-interface data for endophytic holoparasites is sparse, so this
> is the most **schematic** of the five profiles — flagged as such in VALIDATION §7.

## Honest caveats (state these)
- These validate **inputs, not outputs** — the p_c *ranking* is a model prediction
  with no empirical counterpart yet (wet-lab validation = future work).
- The **"efficiency"** parameter (full vs partial vessel disabling) is a *modeling
  proxy* for holo-vs-hemi sink strength, not a directly measured quantity.
- Root parasites (*Orobanche*, *Striga*) attach to the **root system**; our network
  models the shoot vasculature with the root as a single source node, so root
  attack is approximated as "tap the root-proximal backbone."
- For a few paywalled items (Nickrent, Westwood, Yoshida 2016, Těšitel body), pull
  the exact wording from the PDF before quoting verbatim.
